# Theocracy — SE Decomposition

Extracted from `projects/systems-introduction-book/docs/part2/ten-systems.md`
§2.1.3. IDs use the `T*` prefix as in the source.

## Goals (TG)

- **TG1** — Implement divine law as the foundation of all governance
- **TG2** — Ensure spiritual salvation and moral purity of the population
- **TG3** — Maintain order, security, and territorial integrity
- **TG4** — Preserve clerical authority as the sole legitimate interpreter of divine will

## Requirements (TR)

- **TR1** — Sacred text(s) serving as supreme legal source above all human legislation
- **TR2** — Clerical qualification for governance — only authorized interpreters hold power
- **TR3** — Religious conformity enforcement — apostasy, blasphemy, and heresy sanctioned
- **TR4** — Military and security forces loyal to the theocratic order
- **TR5** — Revenue system (religious taxes, endowments, state revenue)
- **TR6** — Education system transmitting religious doctrine as civic education

## Functions (TF)

- **TF1** — Divine legislation — derive laws from scripture and clerical interpretation
- **TF2** — Religious adjudication — resolve disputes per sacred law
- **TF3** — Moral enforcement — police virtue, suppress dissent and heresy
- **TF4** — Military command and territorial defense
- **TF5** — Revenue collection and treasury management
- **TF6** — Doctrinal education — schools and universities teaching approved theology

## Logical Architecture (TL)

- **TL1** — Clerical-Legislative Subsystem — supreme religious authority, jurisprudential councils
- **TL2** — Religious-Judicial Subsystem — Sharia courts, ecclesiastical tribunals, rabbinic courts
- **TL3** — Enforcement Subsystem — morality police, security forces, censorship apparatus
- **TL4** — Military Subsystem — army, revolutionary guard, defense forces
- **TL5** — Fiscal Subsystem — treasury, religious taxes (khums, zakat, tithe)
- **TL6** — Doctrinal Education Subsystem — seminaries, madrasas, schools

## Physical Implementation (TP)

- **TP1** — Supreme Leader / Pope / Chief Rabbi / Council of Guardians
- **TP2** — Sacred text as constitutional document (Quran, Torah, Canon Law)
- **TP3** — Religious courts, Sharia judges, ecclesiastical tribunals
- **TP4** — Morality police (Gasht-e Ershad), censorship boards, inquisition
- **TP5** — Revolutionary Guard / Papal Guard / military forces
- **TP6** — Seminaries (Hawza, Yeshiva), madrasas, religious universities
