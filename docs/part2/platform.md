# The Social System Platform

## Ten Universal Functional Slots

When we align all ten social system decompositions, every system — from kingdom to family, from military to Verein — fills the same set of functional slots:

| Functional Slot | Description | Present In |
|-----------------|-------------|----------|
| **Authority & Decision-Making** | Some mechanism determines who decides what | All 10 |
| **Membership & Belonging** | Criteria define who is in and who is out | All 10 |
| **Resource Acquisition & Allocation** | The system obtains and distributes resources | All 10 |
| **Norm Setting & Enforcement** | Rules are established and compliance ensured | All 10 |
| **Dispute Resolution** | Conflicts between members are adjudicated | All 10 |
| **Legitimation** | The system justifies its authority | All 10 |
| **Succession & Continuity** | The system survives leadership transitions | All 10 |
| **External Representation** | The system interfaces with the outside world | All 10 |
| **Socialization & Onboarding** | New members are inducted and taught norms | All 10 |
| **Activity Delivery** | The system performs its declared purpose | All 10 |

This is not a coincidence — it reflects the fundamental problems that any group of humans must solve when they cooperate beyond a one-time interaction.

The platform is why institutional reform is possible at all: the functional slots remain even when physical implementations collapse, and successful reform transplants implementations from one system type to another while respecting the variation-point boundaries.
