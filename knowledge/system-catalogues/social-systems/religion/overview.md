# Religion — Overview

Religion is the founding case study of this project — the first
social system decomposed through the five-level SE hierarchy and the
worked example that the rest of the book builds on.

## At a glance

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Kind of system | Normative / belief-based social institution |
| Typical scale | From a single village congregation to transnational hierarchies of hundreds of millions |
| Historical span | Prehistory to present; no known society without some form of religion |
| Canonical examples | Christianity, Islam, Judaism, Hinduism, Buddhism, Sikhism |

## Why it is in the catalogue

Religion was chosen as the founding example because:

1. It is a social system that clearly persists across generations, has
   a stated purpose structure (Goals G1-G4), and exhibits emergent
   behaviour the individual members do not.
2. Its structural elements (authority, doctrine, ritual, outreach)
   recur in other catalogued systems (kingdom, theocracy, one-party
   state), making it a useful reference decomposition for comparison.
3. The most interesting failure modes (authority capture, in-group
   escalation) are structural rather than individual, so STPA has
   leverage on them.

See `se-decomposition.md` for the full G/R/F/L/P listing.
