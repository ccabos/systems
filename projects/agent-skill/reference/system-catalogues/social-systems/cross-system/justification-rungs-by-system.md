# Justificatory Rungs Across the Ten Social Systems

A worked comparison of the rungs each catalogued social system
*claims* and the rungs at which it actually *operates*, with the
loop-symmetry profile each combination produces. The technique
this page applies is in
`knowledge/se-techniques/justification-rungs/`. The four-row
profiles are in `control-structure-profiles.md`.

## At-a-glance comparison

| System | Claimed rung | Operating rung | Loop symmetry | Dominant rung pattern |
|--------|--------------|---------------|---------------|----------------------|
| Kingdom | 1 | 0/1 | Symmetric, low | (None — claim and operation match.) |
| Republic | 6 | 3–6 mixed | Deliberately asymmetric across institutions | Asymmetry by design; failure mode is *capture* of any single institution to a lower rung |
| Theocracy | 6 (sacralised) | 1 | Asymmetric (rung 1 down, rung 1 up; rung-3 feedback rejected) | **Pattern B — Claimed-Rung Inflation** |
| One-Party State | 6 (people's democracy) | 0/1 | Asymmetric, claim wildly diverges from operation | **Pattern B — extreme variant** |
| Corporation | 3 | 1–3 mixed | Asymmetric: rung-3 audit at the rim, rung-1 authority at the top | **Pattern A — Asymmetric Loop** at audit-board interface |
| University | 3–4 | 1–3 mixed | Asymmetric: rung-3/4 published outputs, rung-1 promotion and gate-keeping | **Pattern A** in promotion + **Pattern B** in publication |
| Military | 1+2+6 (modern) | 0/1 | Symmetric internally, asymmetric with civilian oversight | **Pattern A** at the civilian-military interface |
| Religion (Church) | 1+6 (sacralised) | 1 | Asymmetric: rung-1 control + rung-3 feedback expected by external observers | **Pattern A** — the dominant failure mode |
| Family | Variable | Variable | Variable | The diagnostic *is* the gap; healthy families close it, dysfunctional families collapse downward |
| Verein | 6 | 6+2 | Symmetric at claimed rung | (None — closest to designed match.) |

## How to read the table

- **Symmetric loops at the operating rung** are the *transmission*
  condition for control — the loop at least *can* function. Whether
  it *should* depends on whether the operating rung is fit for the
  loop's function.
- **Asymmetric loops** are not always failures. Republic's mixed
  rungs are the source of its strength: legislation at rung 6,
  administration at rung 3, elections at rung 1, judicial review at
  rung 2/3. The asymmetry is *between institutions*, not within a
  single loop. Each individual loop within Republic should still be
  internally rung-symmetric.
- **Claimed-rung inflation** (Pattern B) is the most catastrophic
  combination. The wider the gap between claimed and operating
  rung, the more legitimacy is staked on standards the system
  cannot deliver, and the more violently the gap closes when the
  divergence is finally exposed.

## Why the Verein scores best

The voluntary association under German law (Verein, e.V.) is the
only catalogued system where claimed and operating rungs match
across all major loops. Three structural features explain this:

1. The General Assembly is rung-6 by design (deliberation among
   all members) but is constrained by rung-2 statutes (Satzung).
   Members can change the statutes only by rung-6 procedure.
2. Financial oversight is rung-2 (statutory audit requirements
   above certain sizes) and produces rung-3 outputs (audited
   accounts available to all members and to the public registry).
3. The Vorstand (executive committee) operates at delegated rung-1
   authority within strict rung-2 boundaries. Members can recall
   the Vorstand by rung-6 procedure if they overreach.

The result is a control structure whose every loop is at least
rung-2 and whose critical loops are rung-6 — without the
claimed/operating gap that destabilises larger systems.

## Why Republic is rung-mixed by design

Republic deliberately distributes different rungs across different
loops:

| Loop | Function | Rung |
|------|----------|------|
| Constitution → legislation | Set rules of the game | 6 (deliberative) + 2 (formal consistency) |
| Legislation → administration | Implement rules | 3 (evidence-informed) + 2 (legality) |
| Administration → citizen | Enforce rules | 1 (legal authority) backed by 0 (police monopoly) |
| Citizen → legislature | Choose lawmakers | 1 (identity, persuasion) + 6 (informed consent) |
| Court → other institutions | Constrain action | 2 (legal consistency) + 6 (constitutional principle) |

A republic is healthy when each loop's rung matches its function.
Capture lowers the rung of the captured institution: media capture
shifts citizen→legislature from rung 1+6 to rung 1; judicial
capture shifts the courts from rung 2+6 to rung 1; administrative
capture shifts implementation from rung 3+2 to rung 1.

The structural lesson is that *Republic is not a single rung*. It
is a particular distribution of rungs across institutions, with
the highest-rung loops (constitutional, deliberative) constraining
the lower-rung loops. Lose the rung distribution and you lose the
republic — even if the institutions are still nominally in place.

## Why one-party states fail catastrophically

The one-party state combines the highest rung-claim (rung 6,
"people's democracy") with one of the lowest operating rungs
(rung 0/1, party discipline plus coercion). The claim is
operationally enforced — citizens are required to *agree* that the
system operates at rung 6. The result is that the system cannot
register rung-3 feedback (operational reality) at the apex,
because admitting it would expose the rung-claim as inflation.

The Great Leap Forward famine is the canonical example: rung-3
data on harvest yields and starvation existed at every level
below the apex, but the apex's process model required rung-6
narrative to be true ("the masses are achieving record harvests
through socialist enthusiasm"). Cadre who reported rung-3 data
were treated as rung-1 disloyalty cases. The rung-3 information
could not enter the apex's process model until tens of millions
had died.

The lesson generalises: *the catastrophic failures of social
systems are produced at the gap between claimed and operating
rung, not at either rung in isolation.*

## Why religion's Pattern A is the dominant failure mode

Religion (institutional, hierarchical) operates internally at
rung 1 — sacred authority plus tradition. This is honest; it has
worked for millennia. The structural problem is the asymmetric
loop: rung-3 corrective feedback (about abuse, about scientific
findings, about institutional performance) cannot enter the
controller's process model because rung 1 has no slot for rung-3
data.

The architectural fix is **not** to switch the entire system to
rung 3 — that would dissolve the religion. It is to insert a
rung-3 feedback channel that runs *parallel* to the rung-1
authority, with statutory access to the apex of the control
structure. Mandatory external reporting of abuse, independent
financial audit, and lay safeguarding boards are all
implementations of this architectural pattern.

The remedies are documented as rung-elevation moves in
`remedies-case-studies.md`.

## Connecting back to the dangerous patterns

| System | Most likely pattern | Why |
|--------|--------------------|-----|
| Kingdom | (Pattern 1 Accountability Void plus Pattern 2 Self-Sealing) | Rungs match; the loss comes from structural absence of feedback regardless of rung |
| Republic | Pattern A only when an institution is captured | Healthy state has no Pattern A within loops |
| Theocracy | Pattern B + Pattern A | Inflation gap closed only by rung-1 enforcement |
| One-Party State | Pattern B (extreme) | Largest claim/operation gap |
| Corporation | Pattern A | Audit channel structurally rung-3 but boundaried by rung-1 board |
| University | Pattern A + Pattern B | Promotion at rung 1; publication claim at rung 4 |
| Military | Pattern A at civil-military interface | Internal rung 0/1 must be visible to rung-6 civilian oversight |
| Religion | Pattern A (dominant) | Rung-1 control with rung-3 reality of abuse, science, institutional life |
| Family | Whichever pattern collapsed the system downward | Privacy norm prevents external rung verification |
| Verein | (None typical) | Claim and operation match; loops are rung-symmetric |

The full catalogue of rung mismatches and their generic remedies
is in `knowledge/se-techniques/justification-rungs/dangerous-mismatches.md`.
