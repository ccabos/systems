# Democracy (Modern Constitutional Republic) — SE Decomposition

Extracted from `projects/systems-introduction-book/docs/part2/ten-systems.md`
§2.1.2, where the system is labelled "Republic (Modern Democratic)".
The interactive explorer names the file `democracy.html` and this
catalogue follows that convention. Both labels refer to the same
system: a modern constitutional democracy whose head of state and
government derive authority from periodic popular election.

IDs use the `R*` prefix as in the source ten-systems.md (where `R`
stands for Republic).

## Goals (RG)

- **RG1** — Derive governing authority from consent of the governed (popular sovereignty)
- **RG2** — Protect individual rights and prevent tyranny
- **RG3** — Ensure order, security, and territorial integrity
- **RG4** — Provide governance and public services to citizens
- **RG5** — Enable peaceful, periodic transfer of executive power

## Requirements (RR)

- **RR1** — Free, periodic elections with universal suffrage
- **RR2** — Codified constitution with enforceable fundamental rights
- **RR3** — Separation of powers — legislative, executive, judicial
- **RR4** — Independent judiciary with constitutional review
- **RR5** — Professional civil service executing laws impartially
- **RR6** — Free press and public accountability
- **RR7** — Sustainable public finance through lawful taxation

## Functions (RF)

- **RF1** — Legislative process — enact laws through elected parliament
- **RF2** — Executive governance — implement policy, manage ministries
- **RF3** — Constitutional adjudication — review laws, protect rights
- **RF4** — Electoral and accountability administration
- **RF5** — Public finance — budget, taxation, audit

## Logical Architecture (RL)

- **RL1** — Legislative Subsystem — parliament, committees
- **RL2** — Executive Subsystem — head of state/government, ministries, civil service
- **RL3** — Judicial Subsystem — constitutional court, courts hierarchy
- **RL4** — Electoral & Transparency Subsystem — commissions, party system, press
- **RL5** — Fiscal Subsystem — treasury, tax authority, audit court

## Physical Implementation (RP)

- **RP1** — Parliament building, rules of procedure
- **RP2** — Constitution, bill of rights
- **RP3** — President/Chancellor, cabinet, ministries, civil servants
- **RP4** — Constitutional court, federal/state courts
- **RP5** — Election commission, polling stations, party registries
- **RP6** — Tax authority, central bank, audit institution
